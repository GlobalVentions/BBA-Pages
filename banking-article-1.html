How to find the best checking accounts for yourself

The simplest of all account types are Checking accounts but as to whether or not they are lucrative, well lets just say that there are other account types that are far more lucrative than a Checking account. Checking accounts could be likened to an expense account. As an investment vehicle, Checking accounts offer extremely low interest rates in comparison with savings accounts, CDs, and other investment products that would guarantee a good ROI. The thumb rule is Checking account should only be used for daily expenses like monthly bills and daily purchases. Additional funds should be kept in another account with a better interest rate like a CD or a savings account with a high yield. Earlier, people in hordes used to have a checking account at a banking establishment where they had a savings account, mortgage, and credit card as well. Nowadays instances of financial establishments offering the best deals on all of their products are few and far between. In other words, service offerings of banking institutions in terms of financial products have been decentralized.

In the recent past internet banking started issuing ATM cards, checkbooks and paper statements just like a brick and mortar bank except there wasn’t any physical location that customers could visit. These banks saved money on rent as they had a virtual existence and offered better deals instead. Currently internet banking has spread like wild fire among the masses as anybody can avail some of the lucrative deals for savings, checking, and other accounts, more so as fees have increased in traditional banks.

Checking accounts are offered by credit unions as well and they are non-profit membership organizations that replicate the operations of a bank. As they’re exempt from paying as many taxes as a regular bank has to pay, they can afford to be generous when it comes to catering to customer’s needs.

If you do not A Checking account that is adequate is easy enough to find. Banks since time immemorial have established their credibility and have a reputation of proving time and again that they record deposits accurately and their itemized service charges are accurate and transparent without any hidden costs. Hence, its not like one has to pick and choose a bank as services of all banks are standardized. Existing customers can open another account but to open an account at a bank that is nearby would be a mistake. It may be convenient but can be costly in the sense that banks have to pay rent for the branches and added to that there are fixed operational costs as well. Therefore their charges are higher than smaller banks with fewer branches which they pass on to their customers who have to pick up the tab so to speak. Interest rates are lower and deals offered are few and far between.

An ideal combination for opening a primary checking account would be a bank offering the highest interest rates and charging the lowest fees. Moreover, the bank would not have any branches and customers would have to avail telebanking or internet banking services of the bank for accounts-related queries or concern. Internet banks need their customers to mail their checks along with a deposit slip and often times it could take about a week for the deposits to post to/credit customer’s accounts. This option would be perfect for those receiving pay checks via direct deposit and intends keeping some reserve fund rather than spend the entire pay check.

That being said, those who are paid with paper checks like freelancers, handymen and childcare workers who don’t have any reserve fund do need a branch in the neighbourhood to make a check deposit and access funds as quickly as possible.
