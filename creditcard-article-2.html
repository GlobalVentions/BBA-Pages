How to Pounce on Best Credit Card Offers (Before Banks Pull Them)

A lot of people are unaware of the fact that credit card companies actually offer incredibly attractive bonuses while signing up for a credit card to the point that can make some heads spin as its too good to be true. The offer from Chase for example was a huge cash bonus incentive, as anybody would easily get a couple of thousands of dollars just by signing up. Consumer skepticism arises from the fact that hefty bonuses are offered in addition to perks and points that are a part and parcel of daily expenses. Although one may be hopping for the best deals on cards, a signup bonus of a couple of thousands of dollars is in all likelihood impossible to ignore unless there are better offers out there. The banks that are offering these crazy sign up bonuses on their credit cards have stocks that perhaps need to be betted against in times like this.

Once a bank has accepted an application the consumer gets few months for spending couple thousands of dollars. Thereafter rewards points of like 100,000 for example are handed over which can be traded for travel expenses worth a couple thousands of dollars on condition that rooms or flights are booked through the bank. There is a nominal annual fee on the card but part of the annual fee is refunded yearly if travel expenses are purchased through the bank. In addition generous points can be swapped for every dollar spent on travel and dining along with swapping points for miles on most airlines if anyone wants to gamble with availability of seats as part of a reward scheme. There are other benefits as well including using a few airport lounges along with a rebate on fees paid for TSA PreCheck or Global Entry, which expedites international returns.

As these offers are lucrative they are valid for a limited time only. The bonus points are usually slashed after the deadline. Therefore applying online is recommended to avail the bonus points. As the bonus is an initial offer and because banks are quite generous with these types of offers, there is a negative effect on their earnings. Hence banks are going to be less generous with their offers.

Hence, one should grab the offer while it lasts and use the current card judiciously and be mindful of late payments on the new card. Research suggests that people generally tend to pay more when they pay with plastic as opposed to paying cash. A relevant point to consider is whether it would be prudent to discard a card after bonus offer ceases. One’s spending habits could be categorized as dining, travel and groceries for example for purposes of comparing results with a few topnotch rewards cards.

Some points on cards aren’t worth as much as other card points. If points are swapped between cards for frequent flier miles for example, then they could be redeemed for first-class plane tickets perhaps. One should think through their long-term goals and evaluate them accordingly. Those issuing credit cards are becoming increasingly generous with the benefits they offer to have more and more people pour in to sign up for new cards.

Credit check

As always, there are applicable standard disclaimers. The cards should not have any balance on them, as interest charges would gobble up the value of the rewards and more. Moreover, within a short time if one applies for too many cards then that could damage credit scores a little bit.

The crux of the matter is that the response to a new card offer ought to be both appropriate and competitive in the sense that along with targeted bonuses on sign-up there ought to be an array of topnotch services and benefits. Adopting this strategy would ensure delivery of new cards at record levels and along with that, this strategy would also ensure delivery of sustained growth to the company. Offering unimaginable bonuses and privileges to customers though could lead to price war.

Do Not Pity The Poor Bankers

Shareholders and consumers alike are essentially customers of thriving and generous banks determined to being minimally profitable probably do not have any reason to hit the panic button in regards the repercussions of the bank’s generosity.

Large banks can bear the loss of a couple of hundred millions of dollars and consider it as a cost of doing business that the banks will learn from and enhance the product as the banks deem fit. Nonetheless, the crisis of sorts that banks are facing has to do with their very existence. Young adults of an entire generation have been using debit cards as their bank accounts are debited instantly rather than pay later on when they receive the credit card bill. Therefore the banks have to figure out a way of switching these young adults to using credit cards particularly when there isn’t any necessity of paying later for something that they might wan to buy. Excepting fat bonuses and benefits there aren’t other ways of having young adults sigh up for credit cards.
