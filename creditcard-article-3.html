Credit Card Industry Overview – US Market

Credit cards are an integral part of most American consumer’s financial lives. As a major medium of consumer spending, credit cards have accounted for $1.4 trillion purchase in the initial 6 months of 2015. In other words, there were 14.5 billion U.S. general purpose credit card transactions accounting in these 6 months.

Credit cards are also a major source of debts incurred by the consumers. As of the end of the 2nd quarter of 2015, $703 billion in credit card loans were outstanding.

Since the recession, the credit card market has improved a lot. But the credit card transactions are lower than they were and many of the prominent forms of backend pricing have declined altogether. In fact credit lines and approved rates are also increasing. But certain risks are still persisting. Deferred interest in particular is not going great guns.

The credit card market witnessed mixed performance in the year 2015. Although the consumers cleared their credit card debts, yet they spent less money using their cards. Seasonal spending patterns and temporary economic slowdown led to this trend of using credit cards.
