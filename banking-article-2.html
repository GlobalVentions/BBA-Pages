Savings and checking acct- Comparison

A checking account is a category of bank account that is meant for daily monetary transactions earning nominal interest. A savings account however is intended for earning compound interest over time on the money saved in the account. The interest rates on savings accounts are higher than checking accounts and therefore depositing large amounts so as to avail higher interest rates is recommended. Bank charges like maintenance fees every month, minimum balance requirements in the account along with interest rates vary from bank to bank.

Medical cost is Account Fees

The dual purpose of a Checking account is to facilitate direct deposit of paychecks as well as to maintain a minimum balance as stipulated or carry out preset monthly transactions. Account holders also have to pay maintenance fees every month along with add-on bank charges like ATM usage fees, overdraft charges, and as a cover against overdraft charges - overdraft protection fees, net banking fees and bill payment. These add-on charges vary from bank to bank, with a few banks and credit unions charging nominal fees.

As long as owners of savings account remain within their limits of daily withdrawal, banks would not levy any fees whatsoever. However, the criterion of a few banks, like Bank of America, is that owners of accounts maintain a minimum daily balance or carry out monthly funds transfers into the savings account so as to evade maintenance fees of the account.

Interest Rates

Interest earned on Checking account could vary from bank to bank from very nominal to none at all. Interest accrual on Savings accounts however, is common and the rate offered may again vary from bank to bank depending on criteria like the nature of savings account and the deposited amount but one thing is sure and that is interest rates on savings account as a thumb rule would be higher than that of checking accounts.

Since last year, there has been an interest rate hike of about 1% on savings accounts in the United States in particular. Exclusive online banks, that aren’t traditional brick and mortar businesses often, offer special interest rates for accounts with a higher yield. Even credit unions can offer better than average interest rates that most banks offer.

Bill Pay

There are different types of online transactions that are feasible with a checking account. In online banking for example, the owner of an account who may be making recurring payments like rent, water/electric bills, and even one-time payments, could setup automatic bill pay. Although direct debit and standing order payments cannot be made through a savings account but funds could be transferred from a savings to a checking account.

Debit Cards

With a Debit card mapped to a Checking account ATM withdrawal is seamless and with the plastic card items can be purchased in a traditional store rather than with cash. Debit cards are meant for spending money available in the account. In other words, expenditures on debit cards are limited to the balance in the account.

Debit card facility isn’t available on Savings accounts; therefore withdrawals from savings account can be made by transferring funds online to a mapped checking account through a telephonic request or at the bank.

Restrictions

Unrestricted deposits and withdrawals can be made on a checking account.

Savings accounts are meant for using occasionally and there are restrictions on the frequency of monthly withdrawals inclusive of online funds transfers and direct debits. Savings account deposits are unlimited.

Usage

A withdrawal from a savings account at an ATM is feasible but cash withdrawal by ATMs is from a Checking account.
